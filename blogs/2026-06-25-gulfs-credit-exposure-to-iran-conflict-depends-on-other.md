Moody's sector in-depth analysis finds that Gulf Cooperation Council sovereigns' credit exposure to the Iran conflict hinges on the availability of alternative export routes and the strength of their fiscal buffers. Countries with diversified export options and ample fiscal reserves are better positioned to absorb the shock.
